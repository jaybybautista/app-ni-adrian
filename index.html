Documentation

A comprehensive reference for the vehicle emission calculator — covering datasets, formulas, pollutants, fuel types, and methodology based on Philippine transportation data.

01 — About
What is this calculator?

VehicleCarbon computes greenhouse gas (GHG) and air pollutant emissions from road vehicles in the Philippine context. It uses fuel-based emission factors and activity data (distance traveled, number of vehicles) to produce emission totals for CO₂, PM, CO, HC, NOₓ, and SOₓ.

Results can be viewed in daily, monthly, or yearly timeframes and in grams, kilograms, or metric tons, making it suitable for household surveys, transport studies, and environmental science coursework.

03 — Formulas
Calculation Methodology

The calculator uses a straightforward activity-based emission inventory approach. All emission factors are per-liter of fuel consumed.

// Step 1: Fuel consumption rate from economy
Fuel Consumption (L/km) = 1 ÷ Fuel Economy (km/L)

// Step 2: Emission per km for each pollutant
Pollutant (g/km) = Emission Factor (g/L) × Fuel Consumption (L/km)

// Step 3: Total emission for fleet
Total Pollutant (g) = Pollutant (g/km) × Number of Vehicles × Distance (km)

// Step 4: Time period scaling
Monthly = Daily × 30 | Yearly = Daily × 365

For example, a Passenger Car (Gasoline) with 12 km/L economy has a fuel consumption of 0.0833 L/km. At a CO₂ factor of 2,350 g/L, it emits 195.83 g CO₂ per km. With 8 vehicles traveling 10 km each, total CO₂ = 195.83 × 8 × 10 = 15,667 g.

05 — Fuel Types
Gasoline vs Diesel

Emission factors differ significantly between gasoline and diesel fuels. Diesel produces more CO₂ per liter (2,680 g/L vs 2,350 g/L for gasoline) but typically has better fuel economy. Diesel also produces significantly more particulate matter (PM).

⛽

Gasoline

CO₂ factor: 2,350 g/L. Used in passenger cars, motorcycles, and tricycles. Lower PM emissions but typically less fuel-efficient than diesel for heavy vehicles.

🛢️

Diesel

CO₂ factor: 2,680 g/L. Used in passenger cars, jeepneys, and buses. Higher PM (2 g/L) and NOₓ emissions. More energy-dense than gasoline.

🚌

Big Bus (Special)

CO₂ factor: 6,288 g/L — the highest in the dataset. Uses a different fuel blend. Extremely high PM (3.13 g/L) and CO (363 g/L) factors.

🚐

Mini Bus

CO₂ factor: 985 g/L. Lower emission factors than the big bus, representing a cleaner but still significant transport mode for urban areas.
